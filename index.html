📌 What Makes a Proposal Actually Win Business

Lead with the problem, not with yourself

Clients want to know you understand their challenge. Open your executive summary with what they're struggling with, not your company bio.

Break scope into phases they can follow

Numbered scope sections with deliverables give clients a mental roadmap. They stop asking "what am I paying for?" because you showed them.

Include a timeline — even a rough one

A proposal without a timeline feels like a wish list. Milestones show you've planned the work and give the client something to hold you to.

Put pricing at the end, not the beginning

By the time the client reaches the pricing table, they should already be sold on the approach. That's the entire point of a proposal over a plain quote.
